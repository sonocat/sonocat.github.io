Gestational Sac

  • Look for the gestational sac, which should be found at the uterine fundus and within the endometrium.
  • The normal gestational sac is usually seen around 5 weeks.
  • Note any fluid surrounding the gestational sac e.g. subchorionic hemorrhage.

    • Measurement images: Take A/P, length, and width measurements, which are averaged to give the mean sac diameter (MSD).
    Cine: Sagittal and transverse clips through the gestational sac.

Yolk Sac

  • Zoom in the screen and adjust the gain to show the normal yolk sac as a circular echogenic rim with an anechoic center.
  • The yolk sac is the first structure seen in a true gestational sac at around 5.5 weeks (and may no longer be visible after 10 weeks).
  • Diameter <6 mm in early first trimester.

    • Measurement image: Calipers placed vertically from inner to inner rim of the yolk sac.

Fetal Pole

  • Do not use color or Doppler over the embryo.
  • Zoom in the screen, which will help you to measure the fetal pole accurately.
  • The fetal pole is first seen around 5.5 to 6 weeks.

    • Measurement image: Take the crown rump length (CRL) of the fetal pole in the long axis.

Heart Rate

  • Do not use color or Doppler over the embryo.
  • Optimize depth and zoom in the screen, and then use M-mode to measure the fetal heart rate.
  • Heart motion should be seen when the embryo is first visualized, once CRL measures at least 2 mm. It is abnormal when there is still no detectable heart motion at CRL ≥ 7 mm.
  • Normal FHR ≥110 bpm at early gestation up to 170 bpm by 10-11 weeks.

    • Measurement image: Calipers placed on two consecutive peaks of the cardiac cycle on M-mode.
    Cine: Take at least one clip showing fetal heart motion.

Fetal Number

  • When there are multiple gestations in the first trimester, document the amnionicity and chorionicity, and repeat the above for the remaining gestational structures.

Pelvic Structures of Note

  • A regular pelvic protocol is then performed to evaluate the uterus including cervix, ovaries, adnexa and cul-de-sac.
  • Special attention is made to the cervix, which should be closed. See imminent abortion.
  • The corpus luteum should be seen in one of the ovaries in the first trimester.
  • The endometrium is no longer measured.
