Scrotum Protocol

    Foreword on Patient Prep

    The "rolled towel between the legs" method is unnecessary. Use a single blanket, which both supports the scrotum (placed on top of the sheet) as well as covers the patient's legs. As an added benefit to using this method, you can easily pull on one side of the blanket to reposition any internal scrotal structures e.g. epididymis. Steps outlined below:

  1. The patient is supine, unclothed from the waist down, draped with a gown open to the back, and covered with a blanket over the legs and lower torso.
  2. Tell the patient to pull his gown up to the groin such that it is still covering the penis, but the scrotum is exposed.
  3. Uncover the blanket down to the groin, and have him elevate the scrotum or place the scrotum over the blanket.
  4. Ask the patient to keep his legs close together. The scrotum should now mostly be immobile.

    5. Scanning Duration: 15 minutes, not including standing varicocele evaluation.

Testes B/L Trans

  • Take an image showing both testes to compare texture and echogenicity.
  • This can be challenging in cases of large hydroceles. Switch to a curvilinear probe and obtain both testes in one image. When this is not possible, use dual screen.

    • Color image: One image showing perfusion in both testes.

Right Testis Sag

  • Use a light pressure so as not to deform the testis.
  • Obtain the oblong sagittal midline testis.

    • Measurement image: Take the A/P and length measurements.

Right Testis Trans

  • Use a light pressure so as not to deform the testis.
  • Obtain the circular transverse midline testis. If the shape is not a circle, change your angle or lighten your pressure.

    • Measurement image: Take the transverse measurement.

Right Testis Sag Med/Mid/Lat

    Color image: One image at midline testis, showing perfusion in the upper through lower poles.
    Doppler image: Sample an artery within the center of the midline testis.
    Cine: Medial to lateral, or lateral to medial.

Right Testis Trans Sup/Mid/Inf

    Color image: One image at midline testis.
    Cine: Superior to inferior.

Right Epididymis Sag Head/Body/Tail

  • Take images of the epididymal head, body, and tail.
  • If the epididymis is in a posterior position, you can slowly apply pressure to the scrotum until you see the epididymis "flip over". Alternatively, pull on the blanket supporting the scrotum to make the scrotal contents move.
  • Use your color images to compare vascularity between the epididymis and the adjacent testis.

    • Color image: One image each at the head, body, and tail.
    Cine: One clip showing the length of the epididymis.

Right Epididymis Trans Sup-Inf

  • Show the entire epididymis including the ductus deferens as it comes off the epididymal tail.

    • Cine: Superior to inferior.

Right Hydrocele Sag/Trans

  • If there is only trace hydrocele, then skip this.
  • Otherwise, put color to hydrocele and measure it in three dimensions.
  • Document any incidental scrotoliths. Do not measure them unless they are an area of concern.

    • Color image: One image each in sagittal and transverse.

Right Supine Spermatic Cord W/ Valsalva

  • Scan supero-laterally from the testis to identify the pampiniform plexus veins.
  • Steer the color box to the correct direction, and ask the patient to slowly perform the Valsalva maneuver. Ask the patient to bear down as if doing a bowel movement. Sometimes having the patient take deep breaths in and out is sufficient to elicit a flow reversal.

    • Cine: After a few practice runs, limit one cine clip showing good color filling with Valsalva.

Right Supine Spermatic Cord (W/ Valsalva)

  • Measure the A/P diameter of the largest pampiniform plexus vein in the sagittal view.
  • Have the patient perform Valsalva and measure the same vein again.
  • The spermatic cord normally includes the ductus deferens, which will not have color filling. Do not measure the deferens as a vein.
  • Varicocele ≥0.25 cm.

    • Dual screen image: Show the pampiniform plexus before and after Valsalva, and take measurements.

Supine vs. Standing Varicocele

  • If this study is for infertility, hypogonadism, or r/o varicoceles, then perform standing varicocele evaluation for both sides (at the end).
  • Else repeat the protocol for the left side including supine varicocele evaluation only.
